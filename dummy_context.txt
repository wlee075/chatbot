# INCA Platform — Content Moderation Background

## Company & Platform
Shopify-like e-commerce platform called INCA. Merchants list and sell products.
Three merchant tiers:
- RMT (Registered Merchant Tier) — verified, high-volume sellers
- CMT (Categorised Merchant Tier) — mid-tier, category-approved sellers
- COI (Cash-on-Inspection) — unverified, low-trust sellers

## Current Moderation System
- Single uniform review threshold applied to all merchant types
- All new product listings go through a manual + automated review queue
- Current SLA: 5 business days for all merchant types
- Current false positive rate (RMT): 17%
- Average review backlog: 6 weeks (as of March 2026)
- Moderation tool: internal platform called "INCA Ops Dashboard"

## Problem
The uniform threshold penalises high-trust RMT merchants unfairly.
Experienced, high-GMV merchants face the same delay as unverified COI merchants,
causing merchant churn and support escalations.

## Proposed Direction
Introduce risk-differentiated moderation thresholds by merchant tier.
RMT merchants with GMV above $50,000/month (90-day rolling average) should
receive a fast-track review path with a tighter SLA.
